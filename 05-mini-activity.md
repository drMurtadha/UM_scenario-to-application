# Mini Activity: From Weak Idea to Clear Scenario

## Activity Duration

5 minutes.

## Activity Objective

Students practise converting a vague application idea into a clearer problem scenario.

## Weak Idea

> I want to build a food ordering app for campus.

## Task

Improve the idea by answering these questions:

1. Who is the user?
2. What is the current problem?
3. What happens now?
4. Why is it inefficient?
5. What data is needed?
6. What should the app help users do?
7. What is the simplest version of the app?

## Example Answer

```text
Target user:
Students and food stall operators on campus.

Context:
Students buy food from campus stalls during lunch hours.

Current process:
Students queue manually, check menu availability at the stall, and place orders directly with the operator.

Pain point:
Queues are long during peak hours. Students may not know which food is still available. Stall operators may also make mistakes when handling many orders manually.

Impact:
Students waste time waiting, and stall operators may lose orders or serve customers slowly.

Data needed:
Student name, stall name, menu item, price, order quantity, order time, payment status, and order status.

Proposed application:
A simple campus food pre-ordering application that allows students to view menus, place orders, and check order status. Stall operators can view incoming orders and update order progress.
```

## Improved Scenario

> Students on campus often face long queues when buying food during lunch hours. Some students do not know which food stalls are open or whether certain menu items are still available. Stall operators also handle orders manually, which may cause delays and order mistakes. A simple campus food pre-ordering application can help students view menus, place orders, and check order status, while stall operators can manage incoming orders using a simple dashboard.

## Discussion Questions

1. Is the problem clear?
2. Who benefits from the application?
3. What is the simplest possible prototype?
4. What data should be stored?
5. What feature should be developed first?
